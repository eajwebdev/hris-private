## WHY THIS VERSION KEEPS PEOPLE TO THE END

A module tour is a brochure. Nobody finishes a brochure. This is a **story with a villain**.

| Retention device | Where | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold open + curiosity gap** | 0:00 | Shows the *ending* first. "Paano?" — they must stay to find out. |
| **A villain with a dare** | 0:16 | Kuya Boy: *"Wala namang makakaalam."* Now they NEED to see him caught. |
| **The punchline** | 0:40 | He gets caught by geo-tagging. Biggest laugh = biggest share. |
| **Second open loop** | 0:40 | He's humiliated… so what happens to him? Resolves only at 1:12. |
| **Redemption payoff** | 1:12 | Kuya Boy becomes Employee of the Month. Warm, funny, shareable. |
| **Loop closes** | 1:20 | The 5PM answer from the cold open finally lands. |

**Modules are never listed — they are *plot events*.** Geo-tagging isn't a feature, it's the
trap. Recruitment hires the new girl; Events is where she's welcomed. Performance isn't a
feature, it's Kuya Boy's redemption. That's the whole trick.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Sound design is doing half the work. Do not skip it:**
- 0:04 **record scratch** (cold open → flashback)
- 0:22 ominous sting under *"...sabi niya."*
- 0:32 **music swell** on the turn
- 0:44 **comedic fail sting** ("ding-dong") when Kuya Boy's phone flashes red ← *the moment*
- 1:20 music resolves warm on the 5PM exit

**Casting Kuya Boy is the most important creative decision.** He must be *lovable*, not
sleazy. If the audience hates him they feel judged — they know a Kuya Boy, and some of them
*are* him. If they love him, they tag him. Lovable rascal, sitcom energy, always.

**Splice real screen recordings into Beats 6–10.** The prompts show hands and faces on
purpose — never let Flow invent your UI. Your product is real; a hallucinated dashboard is
the fastest way to lose a buyer.

**Captions burned in during edit, never in Flow.** Most FB viewers watch muted, and AI renders
text as garbled pseudo-lettering.

**Campaign setup:**
- Objective: **Messages** (not Traffic, not Engagement — Messages).
- **Auto-reply on the keyword "HRIS."** Half your leads arrive while you're asleep, and a cold
  lead dies within the hour.
- **Pin the Messenger link as the first comment.**
- The caption's *"Tag mo na si Kuya Boy 😂"* is deliberate: tags and comments are the signal
  that makes Facebook push the ad to more people for free.

**Watch the retention graph at 0:16 and 0:40.** If viewers drop before meeting Kuya Boy, the
cold open is too slow. If they drop right after he's caught, the redemption beat isn't teased
hard enough — cut straight from 0:48 to a 1-second flash of his shocked face.
